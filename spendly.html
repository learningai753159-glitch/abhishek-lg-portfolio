Spendly

I didn't find the app I needed. So I built it.

A personal-finance app built around one honest question — not how much you spent, but whether you should have. Designed, built, and shipped by one person.

“

I used to track my expenses in Excel. I'd label each one — Necessary, Good to Have, Unnecessary. It was tedious. Open laptop, enter, calculate, go back and analyse. But that one habit — that one question — was the most honest thing I ever asked myself about money. I just needed a better tool for it.

— that tool became Spendly.

The story behind Spendly

1

Strict questions. Valuable lessons.

My father always took a spending report. It irritated me, but taught me the value of money.

2

A habit that stayed.

My mother still records every expense in a notebook. Notebook → Excel sheets.

3

My own tracking system.

Not just how much I spent, but how wisely I spent.

4

The problem.

Open laptop. Open Excel. Enter, label, calculate. Missed entries. Forgot amounts. Too much manual tracking.

5

One thought.

"What if there was a simple application for this?"

6

The limitation.

I'm a designer, not a developer. Frontend, backend, database, deployment — too much for a personal project.

7

AI changed things.

Learned. Figured out. Built in a few hours. With Claude, Lovable, Supabase, and Vercel.

8

Spendly went live.

Using it daily on my phone. Replaced mom's notebook. Loved by family & friends.

9

Still learning. Just the beginning.

On my way to becoming an AI-UX Designer. More to learn. More to build. More to impact.

01 — And

Everyone tracks. Nobody reflects.

📓

The Habit

People around me — my mother, cousins, family — were writing every expense in notebooks. The discipline was real. The tool was limited.

📊

My Own System

I used Excel. Labelled every spend. Worked — but only if I remembered to open the laptop, enter the data, and do the math. I often didn't.

💡

The Real Question

Every app I explored while designing this told you how much you spent. None asked whether you should have.

06 — Who Uses It

I didn't target a market. I found one.

Who came back

Homemakers managing household budgets. Working women balancing personal and household finances. Salaried middle-class users for whom every rupee has a purpose.

What that means

Not power users. Mindful ones. People who want to spend better — not just track faster. That's exactly who Spendly was built for.

07 — What This Taught Me

Being a founder taught me what being a designer never could.

1

Ownership sharpens every decision

As a designer I could recommend anything. As a founder every call had a consequence I had to live with. That changed how I think.

2

Constraints are a design tool

Not knowing code didn't stop the product. It forced me to find tools that could execute my thinking. Vision drove the build.

3

Real validation is behavioural

Nobody told me it was good. They just kept using it. That silence was the best feedback I got.

4

Small and intentional beats large and passive

12 active users using it exactly as intended is more meaningful than 10,000 passive downloads. I built for depth. That was the choice.

Founder's note

Any company that hires me gets a designer who has felt the weight of shipping something real — who has made product decisions, not just design decisions. I know what it means to own something end to end.
