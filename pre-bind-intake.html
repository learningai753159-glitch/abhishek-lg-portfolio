01 — The Problem

Underwriters were doing two jobs. They should only be doing one.

The old reality

  • Every submission arrived as 3–5 separate files — Excel for policy details, PDF for schedules, email threads for context.
  • Underwriters manually assembled all documents before they could begin risk assessment.
  • Identifying missing critical data meant opening every document individually — across 10+ files.
  • No single place to see the full picture of a risk.
  • Half a day lost per submission to admin — before any actual underwriting began.
  • High chance of missing crucial information — no system to flag it.
  • Unable to process more than a handful of submissions per day.
The Cost

40% of every underwriter's day was lost to non-value-added administrative work — before a single risk decision was made.

The Gap

No intelligence to flag concerns. No structure to guide decisions. Just documents and an expert trying to make sense of them — manually, every time.

02 — The Strategy

One system. Every LOB. Every client.

"If we built this for Aviation alone, we'd rebuild it for every new LOB. That's not a product — that's a cost centre."

Layer 1 — Base horizontal workflow

75% shared

One common user journey from submission entry to policy bind. Works identically across Aviation, Marine, Construction, Property, and PVT. Designed once. Deployed everywhere.

Layer 2 — LOB customisation

Configurable

Label changes, column headings, sector-specific data fields, additional cards per LOB. No redesign. Configuration only.

Layer 3 — Client customisation

Configurable

Feature toggles, naming conventions, email templates, client-specific additions. Layered on top. Still no redesign.

New LOB onboarding: weeks, not months.
New client onboarding: days, not weeks.
Sales team could demo any LOB from existing prototype — closing deals before a single line of new code was written.
Design and dev cost per new LOB: near zero after base was built.

Step 3 — Guidelines feature
An independent rule-book system, designed from scratch.

Underwriters create custom rules — when any submission matches a guideline parameter it is automatically flagged.

How it works

  • Underwriter sets rule: "flag any aircraft built before 2010".
  • Any submission with matching data is automatically highlighted.
  • RAG dot system on submission cards: 3 dots per card (Risk appetite / Advisory / Restriction).
  • Coloured dot = match found · Grey dot = no match.
  • A glance replaces manual scanning of entire documents.

04 — My Design Contributions

Three decisions I made that became platform standards.

01 · Right Side Panel

Problem

Supporting features (notes, attachments, guidelines, audit logs) needed dedicated space. Tab switching felt like changing pages — breaking workflow.

Decision

Introduced a persistent right sidebar to house all supporting features in one accessible place. Eliminated multiple dedicated tabs entirely.

Outcome

Adopted across every section of the platform across all 5 LOBs. Still the core navigation pattern today.

The sidebar houses four states accessible via the right icon strip — Summary, Notes, Attachments, and Updates. Two key states shown below.

03 · Unified Filter System

Problem

10 filters appeared separately — users opened each one individually, applied them one by one. 4 filters = minimum 8 interactions.

Decision

Consolidated all filters into one panel. Left side shows all filters. Right side shows applied filters as tags. Select all at once. Apply once.

Outcome

Reduced filter interactions from 8+ to 2. Applied filters visible at a glance without reopening dropdowns. Adopted platform-wide across all LOBs.

07 — What this taught me
1

Foundation decisions are product strategy decisions

Designing one base system that works for five sectors meant making deliberate constraints early. Those constraints felt limiting — they're what made the system scale without chaos.

2

The user's real job is judgement, not data entry

Every design decision came back to one question: does this help the underwriter make a better decision, faster? When that was the north star, the right solutions became obvious.

3

Design for the designers who come after you

When three more designers joined, they needed to extend the system without relearning it. Designing the handoff is part of the work.

4

A pattern decision made once can live everywhere

The right sidebar. The options bar. The unified filter. Each was a single decision that became a platform-wide standard. That's the leverage of systems thinking.

Designing at platform scale

"I was handed one LOB and no design system. I left behind a foundation that five products and three designers are built on. That is what it means to design at platform scale."
